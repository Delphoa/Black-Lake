## Concise Research Notes

### Problem

A singular spectral measure can admit many exponential orthonormal bases, called spectra, without those bases being classified. The paper asks a narrower but structural question: which Beurling dimensions can those frequency sets have? The Beurling dimension is the critical exponent for the maximum number of frequencies that can occur in large intervals.

For the selected consecutive-digit Moran class, general frame-spectrum theory places any spectrum between the measure's Fourier dimension and upper entropy dimension. The paper establishes that this entire interval is attainable and that no dimension level is represented by only a small family.

### Mathematical Setting

The measure is the infinite convolution generated by integer bases `b_n` and digit counts `q_n` with digits `D_n={0,...,q_n-1}`. Its support consists of expansions

`sum d_n/(b_1...b_n)`.

The theorem assumes

- `r_n=b_n/q_n` is an integer at least two; and
- the bases `b_n` are uniformly bounded.

The upper entropy dimension is

`limsup_n log(q_1...q_n)/log(b_1...b_n)`.

The support's Hausdorff dimension is governed by a liminf expression, so variable-scale sequences can separate it from the upper entropy dimension.

### Main Results

Theorem 1.1 states that for every

`t in [0, upper_entropy_dimension(mu)]`,

there is a spectrum `Lambda_t` whose Beurling dimension is `t`.

Theorem 1.2 states that for every such fixed `t`, the level set of spectra with dimension `t` has cardinality continuum.

The paper also proves that the measure's Fourier dimension is zero and computes its upper entropy dimension. The canonical spectrum reaches the upper endpoint. A lacunary delayed construction reaches zero. Intermediate targets come from reducing digit counts at selected levels, obtaining a regular component of dimension `t`, then adding a zero-dimensional irregular component through a maximal tree mapping. Finite stability keeps the union at dimension `t`.

For `t` below the endpoint, continuum multiplicity comes from binary choices between two large delays on infinitely many complementary indices. At the endpoint, it comes from independent sign choices at every digit level.

### Proof Architecture

1. Use a support-scaling non-decay criterion to show Fourier dimension zero.
2. Combine pointwise/packing-dimension results with an entropy-ball argument to compute the upper entropy dimension.
3. Prove a counting formula for separated integer Moran sets.
4. Import a maximal tree-mapping criterion that turns bounded-tail labelings into spectra.
5. Encode positive integers by mixed-radix words and attach one optional delayed label to each word.
6. Choose all-zero delays for the upper endpoint and linearly growing delays for a lacunary zero-dimensional endpoint.
7. For intermediate `t`, thin digit counts so their cumulative logarithmic ratio has limsup `t`; make retained indices regular and all others sparse.
8. Use binary delays or sign sequences to obtain continuum-many spectra without changing the dimension.

## Synthesis Note

### Concept Bridge

The selected paper can be read as a two-layer construction contract. The regular digit subsystem determines the asymptotic capacity invariant: cumulative retained choices divided by cumulative geometric scale. The irregular delayed subsystem carries the extra combinatorial obligations needed for spectral completeness but is made lacunary enough to contribute zero Beurling dimension. A union theorem then composes the layers.

That contract bridges the three related DEPs. Flag Hardy Operators decomposes an operator into geometry-native scale regimes before recombination. Acoustic Phase Retrieval first restores a missing information component, then applies a Fourier reconstruction engine. FGLE Midpoint Scheme separates a stable analytic target from a scale-dependent discretization whose convergence and uniqueness assumptions must be audited independently. Across all four records, representation structure and correctness obligations are separated before being recombined.

The analogy has a strict boundary. A zero-dimensional correction in a mathematical frequency set is not equivalent to a low-cost software patch, an inverse-problem reference source, or a numerical residual. The transferable idea is role separation plus an explicit invariant, not equality of domains or proofs.

### Potential Implementations

1. `Finite target-dimension constructor`: Validate finite `q_n,b_n` assumptions, propose digit-thinning choices for a requested `t`, display prefix-ratio overshoot, and label every result as a finite diagnostic rather than a spectrum certificate.
2. `Upper-count profile explorer`: Compare maximum interval counts across scales for canonical, lacunary, and mixed finite frequency sets; expose window/range sensitivity and dense-subsequence effects.
3. `Proof-dependency auditor`: Encode definitions, imported results, propositions, hypotheses, and dependency edges for Theorems 1.1-1.2; flag any unverified hypothesis match before formalization.

### Deeper Relationship Observations

1. `Invariant-preserving repair`: The sparse irregular component is allowed to change combinatorial structure while leaving the target density invariant. Acoustic reference sources and FGLE inner iterations play analogous supporting roles only at the design-pattern level: they restore a required property without redefining the principal target.
2. `Limsup governance`: The selected theorem and Flag Hardy scale estimates both depend on worst or selected asymptotic regimes rather than averages. Product tools should expose envelopes and exceptional scales, not compress them into one mean statistic.
3. `Multiplicity behind one score`: Continuum-many spectra share each Beurling dimension. Likewise, a single operator bound, reconstruction score, or convergence order can hide structurally different mechanisms. Registry systems should keep construction provenance alongside scalar metrics.

### Conceptual Similarities

1. `Structured decomposition`: Moran digit levels, flag wavelet scales, Fourier modes, and fractional discretization scales all retain problem-native structure instead of flattening inputs into untyped arrays.
2. `Assumption-sensitive guarantees`: Divisibility/boundedness, kernel regularity/cancellation, measurement conditioning, and mesh/time-step constraints determine where each theorem applies.
3. `Finite evidence versus asymptotic claim`: Numerical truncations can illustrate every record, but the decisive claims concern infinite bases, operator norms, inverse stability, or convergence limits and require separate proof evidence.

### MVP Implementations with Code Mock-ups

#### 1. Prefix-Ratio Inspector

```python
from math import log


def prefix_ratios(q_digits: list[int], bases: list[int]) -> list[float]:
    if len(q_digits) != len(bases) or not q_digits:
        raise ValueError("matching non-empty sequences required")
    log_q = 0.0
    log_b = 0.0
    ratios: list[float] = []
    for q_n, b_n in zip(q_digits, bases, strict=True):
        if q_n < 2 or b_n <= q_n or b_n % q_n != 0 or b_n // q_n < 2:
            raise ValueError("paper assumptions fail at a prefix")
        log_q += log(q_n)
        log_b += log(b_n)
        ratios.append(log_q / log_b)
    return ratios


if __name__ == "__main__":
    print(prefix_ratios([2, 2, 2], [4, 4, 4]))
```

This computes finite prefix ratios in the log domain and checks the paper's basic digit assumptions. It does not compute a limsup from finite data or certify a spectrum.

#### 2. Finite Upper-Count Profiler

```python
from bisect import bisect_right


def maximum_interval_count(points: list[int], width: int) -> int:
    if width <= 0:
        raise ValueError("width must be positive")
    ordered = sorted(set(points))
    best = 0
    for left, start in enumerate(ordered):
        right = bisect_right(ordered, start + width)
        best = max(best, right - left)
    return best


def count_profile(points: list[int], widths: list[int]) -> dict[int, int]:
    return {width: maximum_interval_count(points, width) for width in widths}


if __name__ == "__main__":
    toy = [0, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32]
    print(count_profile(toy, [2, 4, 8, 16]))
```

The profiler approximates the numerator in the Beurling counting formula for finite sets. It must be paired with multiple truncations and cannot infer the infinite critical exponent.

#### 3. Hypothesis Gate

```python
from dataclasses import dataclass


@dataclass(frozen=True)
class Hypothesis:
    name: str
    observed: bool
    evidence: str


def gate(hypotheses: list[Hypothesis]) -> tuple[bool, list[str]]:
    missing = [item.name for item in hypotheses if not item.observed]
    return not missing, missing


if __name__ == "__main__":
    checks = [
        Hypothesis("integer divisibility", True, "finite input audit"),
        Hypothesis("uniformly bounded bases", True, "declared finite bound"),
        Hypothesis("tree spectrality criterion mapped", False, "not formalized"),
    ]
    print(gate(checks))
```

This turns assumptions into visible review objects. A passing finite gate still is not a proof; the evidence field must name the theorem or audit that supports each observation.

### Developer Challenges

1. Prevent finite prefix plots or fitted slopes from being presented as proofs of a limsup, zero asymptotic dimension, or orthonormal completeness.
2. Use logarithmic or arbitrary-precision arithmetic and retain construction provenance, because cumulative products overflow quickly and one scalar dimension cannot identify the spectrum.
3. Model imported theorem hypotheses and source versions explicitly; otherwise a polished UI can silently apply the construction outside divisibility, boundedness, or tree-spectrality conditions.

### Author Challenges

1. Give finite-prefix error or overshoot bounds for the digit-thinning construction so computational examples can be interpreted without replacing the theorem.
2. Expand the continuum-cardinality argument with an explicit injectivity proof for binary-delay and sign-sequence encodings.
3. Clarify which hypotheses can be weakened—especially bounded `b_n`, consecutive digits, and the one-dimensional setting—and identify the exact obstruction in each extension.
