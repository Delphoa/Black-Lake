## Insights and Relevance

The reviewed paper makes representational convergence conditional and testable. Weak affine alignment can imply native-axis alignment only when task-visible nonlinearities, task hardness, minimality, and regularity satisfy explicit conditions. Its upstream zippering results therefore motivate assumption-aware probes rather than unconditional claims that trained networks or biological systems must converge.
