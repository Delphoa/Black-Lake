## Summary of Items

The manuscript reviews CorrKD, a complete-modality teacher and incomplete-modality student framework for audiovisual-language sentiment and emotion classification. It analyzes sample-level contrastive distillation, category-prototype alignment, response-disentangled mutual-information consistency, synthetic missingness, three datasets, seven baselines, and ablations.

## Insights and Relevance

- Missing-modality robustness should be measured by failure mechanism, not only average random masks.
- Teacher/student parity, batch structure, prototype availability, and loss weighting are essential reproducibility variables.
- Small F1 gains need seed-level uncertainty, group-disjoint splits, and deployment-relevant corruption tests.
- Emotion and sentiment inference require consent, privacy, demographic, and consequential-use boundaries absent from the source evaluation.
