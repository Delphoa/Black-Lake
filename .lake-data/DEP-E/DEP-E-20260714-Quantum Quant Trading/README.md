## Summary of Items

The manuscript reviews a two-stage theoretical quantum workflow for high-frequency statistical arbitrage. Variable-time preselection searches for high-condition-number portfolio matrices as a multicollinearity proxy, and a hybrid quantum/classical cointegration test applies quantum linear regression around an augmented Dickey-Fuller workflow. It distinguishes source-reported complexity claims from reviewer interpretation and highlights the changed headline complexity between arXiv v1 and the 2022 journal paper.

## Insights and Relevance

The durable insight is not an established trading speedup but an audit pattern for conditional quantum advantage. The proposal depends on qRAM/oracle access, spectral-distribution assumptions, condition-number thresholds, classical residual construction, and unmeasured loading/readout costs. The closest Black Lake bridges are condition-number-aware quantum linear solvers, memory behavior in time-series reservoirs, and state-evolution-guided structured regression decoding. Together they suggest that representation, access cost, and regime-specific validation must be evaluated before asymptotic improvements can support an implementation claim.
