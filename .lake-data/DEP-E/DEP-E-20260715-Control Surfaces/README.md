## Summary of Items

`control-surfaces.md` organizes a heterogeneous ten-paper source bundle around a common reviewer lens: operational controls work best when attached to the layer where risk or error accumulates. The artifact covers purpose-bound tool privacy, agent-native runtime defense, repository-level coding-agent governance, transition-grounded planning, topology-aware knowledge distillation, iterative long-context organization, photonic quantum classification, toxicity-aware peptide generation, pilot-line spin-photon interfaces, and commensurate Al/Ge materials.

The manuscript includes YAML front matter, source metadata, an evidence ledger, detailed technical synthesis, claim assessment, actual methodology, scope and reproducibility limits, balanced strengths and weaknesses, implementation paths, exactly three exercise paths, a bounded MVP concept, related reading, source references, and a selection/replication appendix.

## Insights and Relevance

The cross-source relationship is not that these papers share one scientific theory. It is that each moves measurement or intervention closer to the mechanism that accumulates failure: trajectories rather than final responses, repositories rather than isolated pull requests, transition deltas rather than prose plans, topology rather than symmetric model placement, latent safety objectives rather than post-generation filters, and fabricated interfaces rather than downstream correction. The artifact also preserves evidence-maturity differences so that conceptual, simulated, observational, in-silico, and component-level results are not mistaken for deployment validation.
