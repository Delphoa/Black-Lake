## Summary of Items

The manuscript reviews original and modified higher-criticism statistics alongside original and modified Berk-Jones statistics. It explains the order-statistic boundary-crossing approximation, simulation validation, sparse Gaussian-mixture power comparisons, lower confidence bounds for the number of false nulls, and a multiple-comparison interval-scan application.

The key finding is conditional: original higher criticism can retain a small power advantage for extremely rare signals at conventional significance levels, but its threshold can become prohibitive under stringent multiplicity. Berk-Jones variants are more stable across the broader configurations studied. The deposit records the random/reselection outcome, dedup checks, complete integrity result, exactly three related entries, and zero source uploads.

## Insights and Relevance

Tail aggregation is part of the hypothesis design, not a neutral implementation detail. A robust discovery gate should preserve the individual calibration assumptions, global error budget, statistic variant, minimum-index rule, sample size, simulation validation, and sensitivity across plausible signal prevalence. PAC Confidence contributes finite-sample coverage discipline; Kernel Equivalence contributes hypothesis-direction and margin discipline; Noisy Poisson Inference contributes model-correction and Type-I stress testing. Together they support a versioned statistical decision receipt rather than an unqualified pass/fail label.
