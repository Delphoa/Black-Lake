# DEP-E-20260714-Structure Aware Systems

## DEP Class

`DEP-E` — Research paper / whitepaper / article.

## Subject

A source-first synthesis of ten 2026 preprints spanning language-agent systems and quantum computing. The review identifies structure preservation—across prompts, trajectories, context, workflows, parameters, and physical interaction graphs—as the common mechanism behind the strongest reported gains.

## Contents

- `structure-aware-systems.md` — schema-complete DEP research artifact.
- `README.md` — deposition metadata and attribution.

## Source handling

The selected source DEP contained a research digest and its README. Ten cited arXiv records and complete PDFs were inspected during review. The PDFs were retained only in the automation's private source cache and are not redistributed here. Two official implementation repositories were inspected; one anonymous prototype locator was inaccessible on the access date. No `.source` payload is deposited because redistribution rights were not established.

## Key insight

Across otherwise unrelated domains, the reviewed systems improve reliability by exposing or preserving the structure that a generic pipeline would flatten: layer activations, action traces, temporal context graphs, deterministic workflow boundaries, spectral subspaces, or native multiqubit phase structure. The reported evidence is promising but heterogeneous and should be treated as a portfolio of design hypotheses, not a unified benchmark result.
