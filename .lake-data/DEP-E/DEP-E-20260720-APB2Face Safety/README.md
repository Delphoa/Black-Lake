## Summary of Items

The manuscript reviews a two-stage system that predicts landmarks from audio, pose, and blink signals, then uses an identity-specific modified Pix2Pix reenactor. It preserves the six-announcer, 23,790-frame AnnVI scope; 256x256 output; reported 75 FPS; landmark-error improvement; and average SSIM, FID, detection, pose, blink, and geometry metrics.

The deposit does not provide generation instructions or assets. It foregrounds the absence of an inspected consent/licensing/demographic protocol for YouTube-derived subjects, the narrow and partly custom evaluation, identity-specific model retention, and synthetic-media misuse.

## Insights and Relevance

Geometry control is an auditable interface but also a misuse surface. Document Fraud LLM shows why detection must be layered and calibrated; Biometric Identity Gaps shows why face realization creates lifecycle and resemblance obligations; AR-Drag Motion shows why modern motion generation also needs temporal evaluation, provenance, and disclosure. The resulting recommendation is a consent-bound, provenance-first evaluation harness—not an unrestricted reenactment product.
