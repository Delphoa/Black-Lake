## Summary of Items

`sim_marl_power_manuscript.md` reviews the paper's stacked-intelligent-metasurface-assisted cell-free massive-MIMO model and its NVR-MAPPO controller. It distinguishes source claims from reviewer interpretation, records the random selection and dedup method, documents the repaired complete-source gate, audits the reported simulation comparisons, and proposes bounded offline tools for reproduction and policy feasibility.

No `.source/` directory is included. The PDF, full-paper HTML, metadata HTML, source package, extracted material, render caches, and private verification records remain local by policy.

## Insights and Relevance

The paper provides a compact bridge between electromagnetic phase control and cooperative multi-agent learning, but its evidence is not deployment-grade. Its plots support faster early NVR-MAPPO learning and higher simulated sum spectral efficiency than selected baselines, while missing code, ablations, uncertainty, hardware nonidealities, communication accounting, and strong optimization baselines limit causal and operational conclusions. The related Multi-Point ISAC, Telecom AI Roadmap, and GPMD Regularized RL deposits connect this work to alternative wireless allocation, guarded telecom policy execution, and sharper regularization/convergence analysis.
