### `moran_spectra_manuscript.md`

Reviews the paper's constructive result for consecutive-digit Moran measures. Under integer divisibility and bounded-scale assumptions, the Beurling dimensions of spectra fill the entire interval from zero to the measure's upper entropy dimension, and every dimension level contains continuum-many spectra. The manuscript reconstructs the canonical, lacunary, digit-thinning, tree-mapping, and sign-sequence mechanisms; separates source claims from reviewer inference; and records the random-selection, dedup, source-integrity, cache, and no-source-upload gates.

## Insights and Relevance

The durable insight is a separation of roles. A regular digit-controlled component determines asymptotic frequency density, while a zero-dimensional irregular component repairs completeness without changing the target Beurling dimension. This turns an existence theorem into a reusable design pattern: tune the coarse capacity invariant, then add a sparse correctness layer whose asymptotic cost is provably negligible.

The result is stronger than interval coverage alone. Each target dimension is realized by continuum-many distinct spectra, showing that a single density invariant leaves enormous structural freedom. It also clarifies why Hausdorff dimension is not always the natural upper bound: for the variable-scale Moran class, upper entropy dimension can exceed the support's Hausdorff dimension.

Exactly three Black Lake bridges sharpen the interpretation. Flag Hardy Operators supplies a multiscale harmonic-analysis proof analogue; Acoustic Phase Retrieval supplies the closest repository bridge to Fourier/exponential reconstruction; and FGLE Midpoint Scheme supplies a nonlocal-analysis example where asymptotic guarantees must be separated from discretization and proof-condition details. These entries are contextual and do not validate the selected theorem.
