## Summary of Items

The manuscript reconstructs interviews and a follow-up prototype study with 22 business professionals. It records the prevalence of spreadsheet-heavy practices, representative what-if techniques, participant reactions, design opportunities, and threats to validity. It translates the evidence into bounded implementation paths for scenario provenance, risk-aware goal seeking, and collaborative review without claiming population-level or causal effects.

## Insights and Relevance

The durable lesson is that business what-if analysis needs decision management, not only faster recalculation. Users must preserve goals, assumptions, constraints, data/model versions, uncertainty, risk, and domain rationale across many alternative paths. Beyond XAI, Judge Conformal, and PAC Confidence show why an accessible interface should be paired with explicit verification and calibrated evidence rather than persuasive point predictions.
