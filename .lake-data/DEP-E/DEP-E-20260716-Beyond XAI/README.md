## Insights and Relevance

The paper's durable contribution is a useful separation of purposes that are often collapsed under “explanation”: end-user trust, expert verification, model debugging, causal understanding, and scientific knowledge validation. Its strongest practical recommendation is to stop treating a fluent or visually intuitive post-hoc account as evidence that a model is reliable. The proposed alternative is an evidence chain built from behavioral tests, expert challenge, audit trails, domain-specific validation, and honest model-centered analysis.

The strongest caveat is evidential. This is a broad position paper supported by a large bibliography and selected empirical studies, not a preregistered systematic review or original experiment. Its conclusions sometimes generalize from a deliberately narrow definition of XAI centered on post-hoc proxy explanations. The formal proxy-model paradox is provocative but remains an author argument, and the paper's own “four symptoms plus eight root causes” wording conflicts with its enumerated two paradoxes, two confusions, and five assumptions. The matching operational note is `.logs/20260716-Arxiv-Beyond-XAI-LOG.md`; the detailed cross-DEP synthesis and code mock-ups are in `.reports/BL-Arxiv-Beyond-XAI-20260716/Report-Mark.md`.
