## Insights and Relevance

Hallo4 joins two reusable ideas: train generative systems on explicit human preferences, and preserve high-frequency motion by reshaping temporal features into channels rather than discarding them during latent compression. The evidence supports metric-specific gains, not uniform dominance. For Black Lake, the durable lesson is to pair preference optimization with protocol audits, independent motion/quality metrics, consent and provenance controls, and reproducible environment metadata. OViP supplies a preference-loop comparison, VideoWeave supplies a latent-video consistency bridge, and AR-Drag supplies an inference-aligned motion-control and safety bridge.
