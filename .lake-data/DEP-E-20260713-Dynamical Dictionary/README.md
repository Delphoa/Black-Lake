## Insights and Relevance

The paper offers a precise bridge between sparse optimization and local dynamical computation. Its feedback-augmented spiking network runs in normal and perturbed configurations; differences in limiting spike rates expose reconstruction and weight-consistency information that individual neurons can use without a globally stored gradient. The numerical study shows convergence on three image-derived workloads and a Lena denoising example, but it does not establish hardware-level efficiency or general feedback stability. Related Black Lake deposits connect this mechanism to reservoir memory, geometry-aware online dynamics, and modern sparse conditional-computation systems. The durable implementation lesson is to treat locality, sparsity, dynamical stability, and measured systems cost as one evidence chain.
