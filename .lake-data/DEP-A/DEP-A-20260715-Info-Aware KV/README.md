## Summary of Items

### `2606.26875-whitepaper-review.md`

This artifact reconstructs InfoKV, which combines recent-attention importance with predictive entropy and layer-to-final representation change to retain cache entries that may matter later in long reasoning. It audits the Forward Influence diagnostic, prompt and periodic-decoding experiments, model-specific budget allocation, reported quality at constrained retention ratios, and the missing end-to-end systems evidence needed to turn nominal cache reduction into a general efficiency claim.

## Insights and Relevance

The paper is useful for cache policy design because it frames retention as a forecast of future utility rather than a summary of recent demand. For Black Lake automation and memory systems, the review highlights the need to preserve the scoring evidence, model and layer configuration, compression cadence, and wall-clock or memory-traffic measurements alongside task accuracy.
