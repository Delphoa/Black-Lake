## Summary of Items

### `2604.04514-whitepaper-review.md`

The review reconstructs the retention-to-precision lifecycle, Fisher–Rao Quantization-Aware Distance, trust-weighted decay, seven-channel retrieval, soft-prompt generation, daemon and hook pipeline, and all reported evaluations. It distinguishes paper reports, external primary evidence, reviewer inference, and new hypotheses. It also records the central evidence limits: the headline LoCoMo result covers 2 of 10 conversations and the best of five rounds; the FRQAD preference task is a fidelity-policy test rather than answer relevance; the mixed-precision recall result falls to 0.680; and the current official repository has advanced beyond the reviewed v3.3.26 release.

## Insights and Relevance

The durable design idea is a retention-and-fidelity control plane for agent memory: stored evidence can age, change retrieval priority, move across representation precisions, consolidate into prompts, and be deleted under explicit policy. The review finds this systems direction valuable but does not accept the paper's biological or general-superiority claims at face value. For deployment, it recommends a protected provenance ledger, source-correlation-aware fusion, lifecycle-tier quality monitoring, reversible derived representations, and explicit safeguards for rare high-consequence facts.
