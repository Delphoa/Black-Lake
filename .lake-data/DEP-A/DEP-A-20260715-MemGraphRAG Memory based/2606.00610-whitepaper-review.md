## 11. New hypotheses derived from the paper

These are reviewer inferences, not established findings.

### Hypothesis 1: budget-aware evidence stability

**Proposition:** If the method’s advantage is mechanistic rather than a tuning artifact, it should remain visible under a matched resource budget and a neighboring workload slice.  
**Motivating evidence:** the source separates a proposed operation from its evaluation settings.  
**Predicted observation:** the direction of the effect is stable while absolute values change.  
**Falsifying observation:** the advantage disappears after equalizing hidden preprocessing or tuning.  
**Minimum test:** rerun the complete pipeline with fixed seeds, matched baselines, and archived configuration hashes.

### Hypothesis 2: lineage predicts failure diagnosis

**Proposition:** Linking source, decision, execution, and outcome records will identify regressions earlier than a final score alone.  
**Motivating evidence:** the paper’s method and evidence have separable stages.  
**Predicted observation:** invalid inputs, stale calibration, and runtime drift map to distinct failure classes.  
**Falsifying observation:** the linked ledger cannot distinguish failures that a final metric merges.  
**Minimum test:** inject controlled drift and compare diagnosis with and without the four-ledger record.

## 12. Deployment and governance considerations

Appropriate use requires a complete source identity, compatible model and data coordinates, validated inputs, and an explicit fallback. Poor fit includes out-of-distribution workloads, missing calibration evidence, stale code or checkpoints, and resource claims that omit transfer or reconstruction. Required safeguards are deterministic preflight checks, append-only audits, public-path sanitization, private-source exclusion, and a conservative fallback when evidence is missing. These safeguards improve observability; they do not certify security, privacy, or correctness.

## 13. Replication and falsification agenda

1. Reconstruct the exact source, model, data, budget, and metric coordinates.
2. Reproduce the highest-impact result with the released or reconstructed pipeline.
3. Normalize hardware, batch size, warm-up, synchronization, and failure handling.
4. Run independent seeds and retain raw task-level rows rather than only aggregates.
5. Test a neighboring model, an out-of-domain workload, and a stronger baseline.
6. Destroy or shuffle the proposed decision signal to test the mechanism directly.
7. Archive configuration hashes, logs, output artifacts, and a negative-result record.

## 14. Durable restatement

The durable lesson is not that this method should always be selected. It is that a constrained representation or decision should remain inspectable from source through outcome. The paper supplies one concrete mechanism and a bounded evidence package. Its long-term value depends on whether later work reproduces the measured gains, clarifies failure regions, and preserves enough lineage to explain why the policy worked or failed in a new setting.
